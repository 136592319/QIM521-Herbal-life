Meridian Tapping Therapy

Basic Principles

Meridians are pathways for qi and blood flow in the body. Meridian tapping can unblock qi and blood, strengthen the body, and prevent diseases. Here are some important meridian tapping methods.

Head and Face Meridians

Temple Area

Location Method:

Located on the temples, between the outer corner of the eye and the hairline

  1. Gently tap with the base of the palm or fist
  2. Tap for 3-5 minutes each time
  3. Until local warmth is felt
  4. Once in the morning and evening

Benefits:

  • Dispels wind and releases exterior
  • Relieves pain and brightens eyes
  • Regulates facial qi and blood

Shoulder and Back Meridians

Shoulder Well Point Area

Location Method:

Located on the top of the shoulder, midway between the neck and the outer shoulder

  1. Gently pat shoulders with palms
  2. Move from top to bottom, inside to outside
  3. 1-2 minutes for each area
  4. Until local warmth is felt

Benefits:

  • Relaxes muscles and tendons
  • Activates meridians and promotes qi flow
  • Relieves shoulder and neck pain

Limb Meridians

Arms and Legs

Location Method:

Located along the arms and legs, following the natural lines of the limbs

  1. Pat gently from top to bottom
  2. Maintain even pressure
  3. 3-5 minutes for each side
  4. Alternate between both sides

Benefits:

  • Promotes blood circulation
  • Improves limb numbness
  • Enhances immune function

Precautions:

  • Use moderate force, avoid excessive pressure
  • Avoid vigorous tapping on empty stomach
  • Avoid abdominal tapping during menstruation and pregnancy
  • Use with caution if you have bleeding disorders
